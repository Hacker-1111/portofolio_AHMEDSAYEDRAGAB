About Me

"Ahmed Sayed Ragab is a contemporary Egyptian writer known for his captivating style that has attracted millions of readers on social media. This popularity led Wahi Al-Qalam newspaper to choose him as the Best Literary Figure of 2023, and he was awarded an honorary doctorate by the Academy of Peace in Germany for his efforts in spreading culture, peace, and love among countries. Additionally, the International River of Creativity Academy, headed by Dr. Ihsan Ibrahim, honored him as one of the most influential figures in the Arab world, granting him the title of Ambassador of Peace and an honorary doctorate in recognition of his contributions. He was invited by Egyptian Channel 2 to appear on the program *Shabab Ala Al-Hawa* to discuss his writings and the role of literature in raising awareness of Egyptian and Arab identity. His videos have garnered millions of views, and his followers and readers have dubbed him "The Knight of Writers." Recently, he was hosted by Al-Shams TV channel on the program *Al-Alam Al-Akhar* with media personality Hend Sabry, where the episode spread widely and garnered high viewership in Egypt, the Arab world, and globally. One of his most famous quotes is: "My study of Arabic literature, especially its prose genres, greatly influenced me. From the beginning of my writing, I tried to link history with literature, blending fantasy with reality. Millions were drawn to my writings, encouraging me to continue and even request printed works. I published my first book, *The Kingdoms' Struggle*, which participated in Arab and international book fairs, followed by my short story collection *The Gate of Ameenakh*, which readers around the world requested. Now, we are on the verge of releasing my new book *The Cursed Kingdom of Rimanous*, and God willing, it will be the most popular and widely read in the Arab world. I still feel that I have achieved nothing of my dreams. What I have accomplished is nothing, and my dreams exceed all boundaries. I am certain that God has guided me to fulfill my dream, and I ask God to make my work weigh heavily in my good deeds and intercede for me on the day I meet Him." Ahmed Said Ragab has also written several articles for various newspapers, including *Daily Press Egypt*, *Wahi Al-Qalam*, *Mubasher 24*, and *Ahram Egypt*. The Moroccan Independent Newspaper conducted an interview with him shortly after he received his honorary doctorate."
